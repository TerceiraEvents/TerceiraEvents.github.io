Angra has a **new literature and history festival** — *Festival Monte Brasil* — being put together for 2026 by the **Dois Caminhos** Association for the Promotion of Arts and Culture.

It's not a single weekend. The organisers have structured it as **three components** spread across the year:

### 1. Residency

Started first. Historian and Universidade Nova de Lisboa professor **Raquel Varela** was in residency until **18 February**, researching Terceira's traditional carnival dances as a candidate for UNESCO Intangible Heritage status.

### 2. Season

Started **late March** and runs through the year. The opening was an *audição integral* — a full replay across two consecutive nights of journalist **Arlindo Gomes's reporting from 25 April 1964**.

### 3. Festival proper

**Late November to early December 2026**, ten days. National and international writers, local authors, oral narrators, and a programme that crosses into music, theatre, painting, and crafts.

### Why now

The 2026 dates aren't accidental. The programme is anchored to three anniversaries that converge this year:

- **50 years of the April Revolution** (1976 → 2026)
- **50 years of Azores autonomy**
- **600 years of the Azores**

There's also a longer-term ambition behind the festival: the organisers want to start a federation of public reading events and pursue a designation for Angra as a **"City of Aloud Reading"**.

### Practical bits

- Funding: confirmed sponsorship from the Câmara Municipal; awaiting decisions from the regional government's RJAC cultural support program.
